~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ Moiré Plasma Effect ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ by Ruud Helderman ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ Welcome to this demonstration of the Moiré Plasma Effect. The background animation on this page is pure CSS; there is NO JavaScript involved. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ Looks great on all web browsers with cutting-edge CSS support. Tested on the latest versions of Google Chrome, Firefox, Internet Explorer and Edge. I noticed a slight flicker on all Windows versions of these browsers; Linux seems to provide a more stable image. Mobile versions of these browsers may be more lightweight, and consequently may totally fail to show the effect. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ The idea of the effect is simple: take two layers of slightly wavy parallel black lines, and slide them across each other. The colors come from a third layer of alternating red and green lines in the background. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ This animation started out as a Scratch project in 2015. I wanted to produce an old-school plasma effect, but the Scratch player was too slow for brute-force pixel manipulation. My approach wasn't very scientific; I just started experimenting with GIMP and Scratch until I finally got something I liked. I was surprised to find you could even use multiple colors; all moiré animations I have seen so far are monochrome. I wonder if anyone else ever tried playing with colors; please drop me a line if you know of any such efforts. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ For even more spectacular moiré effects, google for: moiré animation. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ Plasma effects are really the domain of the 1980s/1990s demoscene. Considering my age, you might assume I was involved, but to be honest, I wasn't. Still, I wanted to keep this demo in style, hence this simple scroller. So here come the obligatory greetings... Thanks for your attention, be good, play nice, don't drink and drive, blah blah, and so long everyone. ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~
